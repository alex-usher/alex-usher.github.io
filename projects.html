Genetic Stocks

Imperial College London | June 2020
  • Description: A multilayer perceptron trained, using a genetic algorithm, to predict stock prices given a file containing historical stock data for a company (the training data). Coded in C and produced by a group of 4 members.
  • Challenges: For this project we had a very limited time scale - around 10 days - and so we had to choose an implementation that would be effective but also implementable within the given time period. We also had to choose suitable activation functions for the layers of the perceptron, so as to avoid vanishing gradients.
  • Improvements: An LSTM network would have been a more suitable choice of neural network for this application, but time constraints did not give time for this. We also wanted to let the genetic algorithm control the weight initialisation of the networks, since two networks with the same dimensions but different weights could have very different fitness scores.
